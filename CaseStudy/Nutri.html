Convert PDF Report to Mobile Report

Ensures that users can easily access and read nutrition report results in a mobile-friendly format.

Role

Product Designer

Timeline

Q4 2022

Result

The design has been implemented by engineering and will be completed in Q2 2023

OVERVIEW

We have genetic test results in PDF format. To make it easier for users to read dynamically, we converting genetic test results from PDF to mobile format.

THE PROBLEM

In PDF format, all content can be placed as it has more space. Therefore, we need to know how users read and prioritize the information in a mobile app. Here are some questions that I have :

  • How the user's reading habits when reading the reports in PDF format?
  • What is the first thing users want to know when they see a report?

VALIDATION

Since we need feedback from users directly, we conducted user research with internal users who have purchased or viewed this Report. Here are the results:

Most useful information is Summary of nutrient, this page is beneficial for users because they know what supplements they should take.

DESIGN EXPLORATION

The Context

In the Report PDF, the layout is divided into 4 sections: Micronutrient, Macronutrient, Food intolerance, and meal plan. Then, each section is further divided into 4 parts: lower, higher, influence, and normal based on the user's body.

In the report, this is the most important page as it serves as the initial page that explains the other pages within it. Therefore, I will try to explain the process of designing this page only.

Phase 1

After obtaining the results from User Research, I tried several layout options for the main display of the report. Here are the results:

Option 1

I divide it based on the categories of micronutrients, macronutrients, food intolerance, and meal plan. This is done to make the users more familiar with the structure, as it is divided this way in the PDF report.

Pros:
  • Makes it easier for users to view reports, just like in a PDF report because it is divided into several sections.
  • Faster navigation as users can directly see the list of contents.
Cons:
  • Takes up too much space due to the tall box shape.

Option 2

I divide it into four categories: lower, higher, Influences, and normal. This categorization aims to help users focus on the specific aspect of their metabolism they want to explore.

Pros:
  • Users can directly choose what they want to see, whether it's lower, higher, or something else.
Cons:
  • The boxes are still too tall.
  • The meal plan section has its own separate part.

From the above 2 options, I requested quick feedback from my designer and project manager colleagues and here are some of their feedback:

  • The report still doesn't quite illustrate what is included in the Report PDF.
  • The height of the boxes is too high, causing them to be cut off and not fully displayed on smaller devices.

Phase 2

In Phase 2, I am trying to explore several options again based on the initial feedback. This is an example of the results of my exploration:

Due to limitations, I am displaying the entire results immediately. So, from the above exploration, it becomes a design like this:

In this section, quite a lot has changed, and I'm trying to convey its visual aspect in the report. Here are the changes:

  • I placed the line chart visuals identical to the report. This is aimed at creating a consistent impression with the report, allowing users to quickly see the conclusions of the results obtained.
  • The categorization is based on the Lower, Higher, Influences, and Normal categories.
  • I also included the Macronutrient Ratio so that users can visually see what they should be eating.
  • Placing the Meal Plan Recommendation on the homepage for users to immediately view their daily intake.

RESULT

After conducting usability testing, I made further improvements to the homepage of the report. Here are the results:

Here are some changes made based on usability testing:

  • Reduced the size of the nutrient diagram compared to the previous version.
  • Changed the result label color in each list to be more consistent with the previous report.
  • Combined the Macronutrient section and meal plan into one section, as they are related.
  • Added a 'More Details' button on the Meal Plan Card to inform users that the card is clickable, instead of relying on text alone.

Overall Result

LESSON LEARNED

Nothing is instantly perfect.

We need to seek feedback and go through the process several times to achieve good results, not perfection. There might be improvements to this design in the future, so we always need to keep iterating.
